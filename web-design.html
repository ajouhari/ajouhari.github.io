The good

Making this finished product - the one you're viewing now - took much time and effort, and has been through multiple redesigns in order to become what it is now. It may still not be perfect, but it is much improved from what it was earlier.

What makes this site good is it's simple and sleek look. This site also has more javascript than the others, enabling me to make eye-catching functions like the type writer animation. Speaking of which, the animations, such as the dropdown animation, is much smoother and appealing, adding to the modern, sleek design.

The bad

The common saying that you learn from from your mistakes is very true in this regard. I started the site with the intention of making it in dark mode; however, upon making it so, I realized how poorly the colour clash with each other. I'd recommend picking your primary colors out before you start with the design.

Another shortcoming of the site is its badly sized elements. I realized the end-users don't need the navigation and buttons to be so large. Toning down the size of some elements in future projects made the site much less clunky and having better usability. Upon loading the site would also display in chunks, which looked unprofessional and outdated.

The ugly

What could make of break a design is the colors you choose. A good rule of thumb is to limit the amount of colors you use to just a few, and avoid using bright, tacky colors; instead, opt for smoother, more vibrant color combinations, like blue and white. That said, I would recommend sticking with only greyscale shades for the text and the page background.

Another aspect that should be noted is the proportion of elements compared to each other. A clear example here is the proportion of the banner compared to the articles in the page. Also take note of the images used, for they could easily take up space and increase loading times, so it best to compress it or use a better file format.
